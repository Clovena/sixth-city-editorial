It's draft week, and soon we'll have clarity on where these top prospects will be starting their NFL careers. With these landing spots comes movement on the dynasty rookie draft board — some players will rise, some will fall, but no matter what, there's always room for a mock.

---

**1.01: Jeremiyah Love, RB-ND**

*Newfoundland Blowers via NNY*

We mocked Carnell Tate to Newfoundland, and we mocked Fernando Mendoza to Newfoundland. Jeremiyah Love is probably most deserving of the first overall pick, and the Blowers have an unreliable backfield at present with Travis Etienne and Rico Dowdle, both in new homes. Love would provide an immediate boost to the RB room.

---

**1.02: Carnell Tate, WR-OSU**

*Vancouver Mounties*

Vancouver would love to see the Notre Dame running back fall to 2, but in this mock it's not the case. Instead, they pivot to the presumptive WR1 in the class, hoping to right the ship after the Travis Hunter debacle at the 1.02 a year ago. Tate should be a blue-chip piece right away for a team in the depths of a rebuild.

---

**1.03: Makai Lemon, WR-USC**

*Choccolocco Railfans*

The Railfans appear to be making a push to compete in 2026 after back-to-back years in the doldrums. They've made some strong additions so far this offseason, and they'll also enjoy a top-three pick in this draft. Choccolocco is probably set in the backfield with Jacobs, Brown, and Hampton, so a WR like Lemon would slot in as a strong second option behind the newly-acquired Egbuka. 

---

**1.04: Jadarian Price, RB-ND**

*Iqaluit Trout*

Iqaluit probably has needs in multiple places, particularly as star players like Saquon and Kelce continue to age. But Alec Pierce is, according to the Colts, a top-six receiver in the NFL, so perhaps the Trout will rely on him to lead their WR room in 2026 and beyond. If that's the case, Jadarian Price may be the pick to slot in as a RB2 this coming season and ideally a RB1 when Saquon finally hangs it up. 

---

**1.05: Jordyn Tyson, WR-ASU**

*Gold Coast Sharks*

Jordyn Tyson's injury concerns remain, but he is suddenly gaining steam in the NFL sphere. Recent mocks have him as a strong riser, indicating that he may follow suit in the fantasy sphere. So long as Tyson remains on the field in the NFL, he could support a Gold Coast WR room that is peppered with unknowns.

---

**1.06: KC Concepcion, WR-TAM**

*Mississauga Mastodons*

Mississauga's lineup is starting to become quite stale, with guys like Gainwell, Njoku, and Keon Coleman shedding value in dynasty formats. The 6th overall pick is not going to be quite early enough to land them a bona fide difference maker, but conventional wisdom would have them entering this full-scale rebuild with a wide receiver. The Mastodons have lately shown interest in Concepcion, and 1.06 isn't too rich for him in a weaker draft class. 

---

**1.07: Jonah Coleman, RB-WASH**

*Sarnia SeaSnakes via TOR*

Fresh off the most successful season in Sarnia's history, the SeaSnakes will be looking for ways to further boost their potent offense and make another push towards a championship. They are certainly set at QB with Allen and Hurts; they are quite probably set at WR with London and Odunze; so making the luxury pick at RB makes sense enough here. Jonah Coleman projects as a Day 2 selection this weekend.

---

**1.08: Mike Washington, RB-ARK**

*New New York Benders via WPG*

The Benders have a shockingly strong lineup in most phases of the game after a productive offseason, but their RB room still needs someone to complement Javonte Williams. There is no sure thing down here in the rookie draft, but Mike Washington projects as a second-round selection, and if he lands somewhere favorable like Seattle, he could be a fantasy difference-maker.

---

**1.09: Fernando Mendoza, QB-IU**

*Lower City Lasercats*

The Lasercats stop the slide of the assumed first overall pick on Thursday night. They have their QB1 of the next half-decade or more in Bo Nix, but they will need a plan for after Matthew Stafford retires. Lower City has the luxury of waiting out Fernando Mendoza in 2026, allowing him to sit on the fantasy bench for a year while he learns the ropes of the pro game.

---

**1.10: Emmett Johnson, RB-NEB**

*Sarnia SeaSnakes*

It's easy to envision Sarnia double-dipping at RB, as most of their shortcomings in 2025 stemmed from the ground game. Drafting another rookie runner alongside Price allows the SeaSnakes to hedge their bets; if one doesn't quite hit right away, the Eels have a backup option. And surely they won't BOTH be busts, right? 

---

**1.11: Kenyon Sadiq, TE-ORE**

*Lower City Lasercats via NFD*

Lower City is faced with an aging tight end room. Davis Allen is currently the youngest TE in Connecticut, and he's not likely to make a difference any time soon. Bringing in a talent like Kenyon Sadiq could immediately give the Lasercats a new level of pop at a premium position. 

---

**1.12: Omar Cooper, WR-IND**

*Choccolocco Railfans via TBY*

Omar Cooper is another recipient of some positive pre-draft press lately, with rumors that he could leapfrog Denzel Boston et al into the first round. The way the board has fallen, the Railfans may opt to double dip at WR with a player like Cooper in order to add depth if any of DK, QJ, or Franklin starts to falter. 

---

**1.13: Eli Stowers, TE-VAN**

*Choccolocco Railfans via PEI*

Down here past the top 12, there's not a ton of immediate opportunity. But Choccolocco, having addressed the wide receiver room, may look towards their thin TE room. Mark Andrews is getting no younger, and who knows what Charlie Kolar can be. Eli Stowers projects as the second TE of the class, and he can be solid depth at least, and a future starter for the Railfans at best. 

---

**1.14: Denzel Boston, WR-WASH**

*Newfoundland Blowers via BKB*

Newfoundland has a better setup with Love at the 1.01 followed by a WR at the end of the first, rather than trying to go Tate at 1.01 and hunting for a RB down here. The WR class is much deeper, and so even if Denzel Boston doesn't flourish in the NFL, he'll probably be more productive in fantasy than whatever RB6 the Blowers opt for.
