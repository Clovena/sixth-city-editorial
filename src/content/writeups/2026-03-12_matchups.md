In 2026, five new Bowl Games will be established. After introducing Bowl Games in the 2024 season, franchise pairings have been reworked to emphasize relationships between owners, rather than geographic or historical significance. These new Bowl Games will activate another level of competition and fierceness across the league.

---

**The Atlantic Bowl**

*Newfoundland Blowers vs Prince Edward Islanders*

The Blowers and Islanders have been officially meeting in the Atlantic Bowl since 2024. They represent provinces separated by the frigid water and wind-swept coasts of the Gulf of St Lawrence. PEI has been a consistent HCC presence, with five playoff appearances in five seasons, and most recently a championship appearance; NFD has had their ups and downs, from a dismal 1-13 season to becoming a Cinderella team in two different postseasons, and are always a thrill to follow. 

Two formal Atlantic Bowls have been played; the former Pokémon Omega Judge Judy (now PEI) won the inaugural game in 2024, while Newfoundland exacted revenge in 2025. However, their all-time head-to-head history favors the Islanders by a count of 5 wins to 1. Their first meeting was in Week 2 of the 2021 season, while their most recent meeting was in the playoff semifinals of the 2025 campaign. Prince Edward Island would win that game to advance to their first-ever championship game.

These two franchises are all too familiar with one another, even though they compete across conferences and even though the games tend to end in the same way most years. Even despite this, though, ownership in Newfoundland and Prince Edward Island have a mutual respect and a mutual competitiveness that they bring to every Atlantic Bowl.

**The Blue-Collar Classic**

*Choccolocco Railfans vs Iqaluit Trout*

The Railfans and Trout have been formally competing in the Blue-Collar Classic since 2024.  The two organizations are united by a working-class ethos: Iqaluit toils on the fishing docks in the extreme north; Choccolocco (Alabama) grinds away on the railroad tracks in the deep south. Both franchises represent proud industrial heritages; both franchises have moments of success in the HCC; and both franchises are trying to claw their way back to an ever-elusive former glory.  

Two official Blue-Collar Classics have been held, with the record split at 1 win apiece. However, across five total meetings, Choccolocco has won four since 2021. The Trout's sole victory was in 2024, Week 14, to close the regular season and put an exclamation point on Iqaluit's sensational 11-3 season and postseason 1-seed. 

These two owners share history that extends well beyond the inception of Sixth City Dynasty, making this Bowl Game rich with narratives and always exciting to watch. 

---

**The Atom Bowl**

*Bikini Bottom Stars vs Winnipeg Wranglers*

The Atom Bowl is the first of five Bowl Games being established in 2026. It features two premier franchises in the HCC and in Sixth City as a whole: the perennial contender Winnipeg Wranglers, and the two-time champion Bikini Bottom Stars. The game gets its name from the shared nuclear history of the two regions: the Stars' home of Bikini Atoll is one of the most famous sites of nuclear testing during the Cold War, while Winnipeg was once home to one of the biggest nuclear testing laboratories in Canada. 

The Stars and Wranglers have matched up a total of seven times in five seasons, with the all-time record tilting 5-2 to Bikini Bottom. Two of these meetings have occurred in the playoffs, with Winnipeg triumphing in 2022 en route to their championship and Bikini Bottom taking the win in 2023 en route to their championship. In short, the games between these two have always carried such significance, and the results of these games have always shaped the landscape of the league.

**The Cascade Cup**

*Lower City Lasercats vs Mississauga Mastodons*

The Cascade Cup is new in 2026 and will feature the former rivals in Mississauga and Lower City. The Cup itself takes its name from the waterfalls common to each team's region. The Bowl Game programming acknowledges how these franchises can often be overlooked, both in the scope of Sixth City and in their geographical regions at large. Mississauga lives in the shadow of a larger Canadian metro, and Lower City remains permanently overlooked in the small state of Connecticut.

Three total matches have been played between these franchises since Lower City was established in 2024; and the Lasercats have taken all three of them. Extending the historical results out to include the Calgary Cocks, LOW's former identity, yields three wins to Mississauga and two to the former Cocks. 

These two HCC competitors share plenty of common ground, from their underdog mentalities to their waterfall backdrops and through the rivalry between the owners themselves. If the Atom Bowl shapes the narrative at the top of the conference, the Cascade Cup is sure to dictate how everybody else in the HCC will shake out.

**The Dominion Classic**

*Toronto Hogs vs Vancouver Mounties*

* New in 2026
* Named from the formal "Dominion of Canada"
* Showcases the cross-country rivalry between Toronto and Vancouver as the country's greatest city
* All-SCC clash; therefore also represents the old guard of Vancouver vs the new guard of Toronto in the conference
* Historical matchups reflect this trend: Vancouver was 4-0 from 2021 through 2023; Toronto since 2-0 in 2024 and 2025
 
---

**The Gateway Cup**

*Gold Coast Sharks vs Thunder Bay Mustangs*

* New in 2026
* Name derived from geographical place each city holds in the greater region
  * Thunder Bay as a natural gateway to western Canada
  * Gold Coast as a proxy gateway to the Great Barrier Reef
* Two SCC teams that have seen moments of success thwarted on the biggest stages
* Though TBY is the more successful franchise across history, GLD leads the all-time series between the two 4-2
 
---

**The Animation Altercation**

*New New York Benders vs Sarnia SeaSnakes*

* New in 2026
* Two SCC teams with references to cartoon worlds
  * Benders draw from Futurama
  * SeaSnakes draw from Spongebob
* Bowl Game name inspired by Fox's Animation Domination
* Only Bowl Game with two adopted franchises
* Since 2024, when both teams existed in their current states, Sarnia is 2-0
  * All time, including previous franchise histories, the MTL-SAR franchise is up 5-2 versus the SSK-NNY franchise
